### Manufacturing Process Flow

```
STAGE 1          STAGE 2          STAGE 3          STAGE 4           STAGE 5
─────────       ─────────       ─────────       ─────────         ─────────
 CNC Slitting    Tube Forming    HF Welding     Heat Treatment    QC & Packaging
 & Cutting       Mill            Station        Furnace           Station
   │                │                │               │                │
   ▼                ▼                ▼               ▼                ▼
 Steel coils    Flat strips     Open seam       Welded pipes     Finished pipes
 → Flat strips  → Round tube    → Welded tube   → Normalized     → Inspected
                                                  pipes            → Packed
                                                                   → Dispatched
```

**Process Description:**

1. **Stage 1 — CNC Slitting & Cutting (Machine: CNC-101)**
   - Steel coils (HR/CR) received from storage are loaded onto the decoiler
   - CNC slitting machine cuts coils into precise-width flat strips
   - Strips are edge-trimmed and fed to the accumulator for continuous feeding

2. **Stage 2 — Tube Forming Mill (Machine: FRM-201)**
   - Flat strips pass through a series of forming rollers
   - Progressive forming converts flat strip into an open-seam round tube
   - Fin pass rollers achieve final shape and diameter accuracy

3. **Stage 3 — High-Frequency Welding Station (Machine: WLD-301)**
   - Open seam edges are heated by high-frequency induction coil (200-400 kHz)
   - Squeeze rollers press heated edges together forming a forge weld
   - External and internal weld bead is scarfed (trimmed)
   - Inline weld inspection using eddy current testing

4. **Stage 4 — Heat Treatment Furnace (Machine: HTR-401)**
   - Welded pipes pass through normalizing furnace at 880-920°C
   - Controlled cooling in air-cooling bed
   - Stress relief and grain structure normalization
   - Pipe straightening through multi-roll straightener

5. **Stage 5 — Quality Control & Packaging (Machine: PKG-501)**
   - Hydrostatic pressure testing (as per BIS requirements)
   - Dimensional inspection (OD, wall thickness, length, ovality)
   - Visual inspection and surface quality check
   - Automatic bundling, strapping, and tagging
   - Weight verification and dispatch preparation
