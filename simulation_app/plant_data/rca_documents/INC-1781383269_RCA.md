## Executive Summary

The incident involved a severe failure of an Arbor Bearing in CNC-101A machine's slitting head on June 14th, 2026 at 02:12:09 UTC. The failure was initially acknowledged automatically and recorded immediately. Following the initial acknowledgement, a manual visual inspection revealed no visible defects. However, an automated Root Cause Analysis (RCA) identified multiple contributing factors leading to this critical failure. These included inadequate maintenance of the bearing over time, worn-out lubrication, and environmental exposure to extreme temperatures.

### Key Findings:
1. **Inadequate Maintenance**: Bearings need regular inspection and maintenance.
2. **Lubricant Failure**: Lack of proper lubrication contributed significantly to wear issues.
3. **Environmental Conditions**: Excessive temperature fluctuations accelerated the bearing’s degradation over time.

## Timeline of Events

- 06:12 UTC on June 14, 2026: CNC-101A machine reported a severe vibration issue in the slitting head area.
- 07:12 UTC: Alert was acknowledged automatically. Incident record logged to system database at this time.
- 08:12 UTC: A manual visual inspection of the slitting head revealed no obvious signs of damage or wear.
- 09:12 UTC: Automated Root Cause Analysis (RCA) initiated, focusing on immediate corrective actions.

## Root Cause

### Direct Causes:
1. **Lack of Regular Maintenance**: Bearings were not inspected and serviced according to the prescribed maintenance schedule.
2. **Insufficient Lubrication**: The bearings lacked proper lubricant application leading to increased friction and wear over time.

### Contributing Factors:
1. **Excessive Temperature Variability**: Unstable environmental conditions, particularly temperature fluctuations, accelerated bearing degradation beyond normal tolerances.
2. **Over-Time Use Without Proper Inspection and Maintenance**: This prolonged use led to premature failure of the bearings due to fatigue from repeated stresses.

## Corrective Actions

### Immediate Fixes:

- **Regular Maintenance Schedule Implementation**: The maintenance schedule for the slitting head was updated with stricter guidelines, including regular bearing checks and lubrication.
- **Lubricant Reapplication Program**: A new lubrication protocol was developed, ensuring that bearings are properly maintained even in challenging environmental conditions.

## Preventive Recommendations

### Operational Changes:
1. **Temperature Monitoring System Integration**: The use of a temperature monitoring system to alert when temperatures deviate from normal operating ranges.
2. **Comprehensive Training for Operators**: A refresher course on the importance of bearing maintenance and proper lubrication techniques.
3. **Periodic Bearing Testing**: Implement periodic testing programs, especially after periods of extreme environmental exposure.

### Equipment Changes:
1. **Upgrade to High-Temperature Bearings**: Considering the nature of the operational environment, upgrading existing bearings to higher temperature-resistant models might mitigate future failures.
2. **Remote Monitoring Systems**: Integrate remote monitoring systems for real-time detection and alerting of bearing issues before they escalate.
