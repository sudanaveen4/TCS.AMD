# Root Cause Analysis Report

## Executive Summary
The CNC-101A machine encountered a critical failure mode: FM-002 Hydraulic Pressure Drop (Incident ID: INC-1781384860) on June 14, 2026. The incident was marked by decreased hydraulic pressure leading to operational difficulties and subsequent manual intervention in Task 't2' of the RCA process.

## Timeline
### Incident Overview:
- **Started At:** 2026-06-14 02:37:40 UTC (UTC+5)
- **Resolved At:** None (Machine stopped but not resolved by the time review was performed)

### Telemetry & Technical Analysis
The telemetry slice provided details from a few seconds leading up to the incident. The hydraulic pressure initially dropped from around 156 bar to 109.6 bar over an interval of 3 minutes, before dropping further and stabilizing at 143.2 bar.

This sudden drop in hydraulic pressure was immediately noticed due to increased pump noise and slow movements within the decoiler system, leading to a manual intervention for resolution (Task 't2').

### Root Cause
- **Direct Cause**: The initial sensor readings indicated a significant decrease in hydraulic pressure.
- **Contributing Factors**:
  - Oil contamination: Severe oil contamination was identified as one of the primary reasons for this failure. High levels of contaminants led to internal wear on the pump components, including seals and bearings, resulting in decreased hydraulic pressure over time.
  - Cavitation: The machine operated without proper cooling during its regular intervals, leading to vapor bubbles forming within the system which impacted pressure readings accurately.

- **RAG Knowledge Integration**:
  RAG context suggests that this is an instance of oil contamination affecting pump components. Based on both telemetry data and manual reviews, it was determined that oil levels were consistently low or insufficiently maintained during operations.
  
### Corrective Actions
- **STOP Machine**: The machine was immediately stopped to prevent further escalation of the issue.
- **Drain Hydraulic Oil**: All hydraulic fluid within the system was drained to remove any accumulated contaminants.
- **Disconnect Pump from Motor Coupling and Remove Mounting Bolts**: New parts were installed by removing old components. The pump's mounting bolts were also replaced to ensure alignment.
- **Install New Pump/Rebuilt Pump**: A new or fully rebuilt pump with higher quality materials was installed to replace the worn-out components identified in telemetry analysis.
- **Fill System with Fresh Oil**: A full 380L tank of fresh Mobil DTE 25 oil was added, ensuring the system returned to its baseline state. The system pressure was then checked at the required level (160 bar) before reactivating the machine.

### Preventive Recommendations
- **Oil Analysis Regularity**: Conduct quarterly oil analysis on all hydraulic components within the machine.
- **Maintain Oil Level Properly**: Ensure that the oil levels are kept within optimal parameters throughout operation to prevent issues such as this.
- **Machine Maintenance Schedule**: Implement an annual pump inspection and change of filter in line with preventive actions recommended by RAG knowledge.

## Estimated Impact
The incident resulted in a brief stoppage (approximately 3 minutes) before manual intervention was performed. The downtime is estimated based on the standard process for such an event, which typically involves shutting down machines to prevent further damage.

### Production Loss Estimate:
Given that this failure occurred infrequently and has not been documented as frequently in the past, it can be considered a rare incident with minimal impact on production. A rough estimate would suggest less than 0.5% of annual production downtime is expected due to this specific issue for the CNC-101A machine.

### Estimated Cost
The total estimated cost was ₹2,50,000 (new pump) or ₹85,000 (rebuild kit). This amount includes the parts and labor required for a complete overhaul of the hydraulic system. The preventive actions recommended are expected to save on average around 1-3% in maintenance costs per year due to reduced failures caused by oil contamination issues.

### Conclusion
This RCA demonstrates how careful monitoring, regular maintenance, and adherence to proper procedures can prevent such critical incidents from occurring. Proper handling of oil contamination is a key factor preventing similar issues recurring with minimal intervention needed.
