## Root Cause Analysis (RCA) Report

### Executive Summary
The incident on CNC-101A machine identified the failure mode FM-001 Arbor Bearing Wear at 2:10:32 AM on June 14, 2026. The incident involved a significant vibration and hydraulic pressure anomaly in the slitting head area of the machine, leading to an automated acknowledgment and visual inspection which confirmed wear on the bearing that required immediate attention.

### Root Cause Analysis
1. **Direct Causes**:
   - The root cause for FM-001 Arbor Bearing Wear is identified as an excessively worn bearing in the slitting head area.
   
2. **Contributing Factors**:
   - Long-term exposure to vibration due to machinery operation exacerbated wear on the bearing.
   - Lack of timely maintenance, leading to insufficient time between inspections and resulting in early failure.

### Corrective Actions
1. **Immediate Fixes Applied**: The machine was shut down immediately after recognizing the fault, and a detailed preventive maintenance plan was initiated for all CNC machines. A thorough inspection program was established with increased frequency to ensure bearing integrity.
   
2. **Future Prevention Measures**:
   - Implement a comprehensive predictive maintenance system that includes regular vibration monitoring to alert when bearings might fail before they do.
   - Establish a structured training program for operators and technicians emphasizing the importance of timely inspections and proper maintenance.

### Estimated Impact
1. **Downtime Hours**: The shutdown time resulted in a scheduled downtime of 2 hours, with no additional downtime anticipated due to the preventive measures taken.
   
2. **Production Loss Estimation**: Based on the loss of output during this period and assuming normal machine run times, an estimated production loss is approximately 1% for that day.

### Conclusion
This RCA highlights the importance of proactive maintenance practices to prevent costly failures before they occur, emphasizing the need for continuous monitoring and preventive actions.
