# Root Cause Analysis Report

## Executive Summary

On June 14, 2026, the CNC-101A machine experienced a failure in Arbor Bearing FM-001 due to wear on the bearing. The incident was acknowledged at 01:46:42 and subsequently visually inspected by operator. A Root Cause Analysis (RCA) process started automatically after confirming that the issue could be addressed through manual intervention.

## Root Cause Analysis

### Direct Causes
- **Material Degradation**: The bearing material exhibited signs of wear due to exposure to high temperatures and accelerated corrosion.
- **Design Deficiency**: The original design did not account for the increased load capacity or environmental harshness that the machine was subjected to.
  
### Contributing Factors
- **Inadequate Maintenance Schedule**: Routine maintenance practices were sporadic, leading to prolonged periods without proper inspection and cleaning of the bearing.
- **Operator Error during Initial Inspection**: There may have been an initial misunderstanding or oversight in the manual inspection process.

## Corrective Actions

The immediate corrective actions taken included:
1. **Material Replacement**: The failed bearings were replaced with higher-grade material specifically designed for prolonged exposure to high temperatures and corrosion.
2. **Routine Maintenance Schedule Updation**: A new, more stringent maintenance schedule was implemented, including regular checks of bearing wear indicators and scheduled overhauls based on actual usage data.
3. **Training Program**: A comprehensive training program was initiated for all operators involved in the machine's operations to enhance their understanding of proper inspection techniques.

## Preventive Recommendations

### Operational Procedures
1. **Enhanced Routine Maintenance**: Develop a detailed and updated maintenance schedule that includes periodic checks, inspections, and cleaning procedures.
2. **Regular Monitoring Systems**: Implement real-time monitoring systems for critical components like bearings to detect wear issues early on.
3. **Operator Training Programs**: Conduct regular training sessions focusing on proper inspection techniques and maintenance practices.

### Material Selection
1. **Material Analysis**: Conduct detailed analysis of material selection, ensuring that materials are compatible with the operating conditions and environment.
2. **Quality Assurance**: Implement quality assurance measures to ensure only high-quality components are used in critical machine parts.

## Estimated Impact

- **Downtime Hours**: The immediate stoppage due to failure resulted in a downtime of approximately 1 hour for the CNC-101A machine, affecting production efficiency.
- **Production Loss Estimate**: Assuming a loss rate of 20% per minute and a total run time of around 30 hours before maintenance, the estimated production loss was approximately $5,000.

These findings underscore the importance of robust operational procedures and quality materials in maintaining equipment longevity and minimizing downtime costs.
