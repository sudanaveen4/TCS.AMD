### Telemetry & Technical Analysis
The telemetry slice provided shows a gradual increase in vibration values leading up to the failure, followed by a decrease around the time of failure itself. The pressure readings are stable during this period but suggest potential for overheating due to prolonged bearing wear.

### Root Cause

#### Direct Causes:
1. **Lubrication Missed**: Inadequate lubrication was detected by vibration monitoring.
2. **Metal Fines Contamination**: Metal fines from the material fed into the machine were found in the bearings, leading to increased friction and heat generation.
3. **Overloading**: The machine was operating above its design capacity, exacerbating bearing wear.
4. **Age-Related Fatigue**: Bearings had reached their service life due to continuous use.

#### Contributing Causes:
1. **Machine Overload**: Overloaded system components can lead to increased mechanical stress and wear on bearings.
2. **Poor Maintenance Practices**: Inadequate regular maintenance, including lack of lubrication checks and timely replacements, contributed to the failure.
3. **Material Quality Issues**: Contamination from metal fines in the material fed into the machine exacerbated bearing wear.

### Corrective Actions

#### Immediate Fixes:
- STOP machine immediately at 02:24:39 AM
- Apply LOTO (Lockout Tag Out)
- Remove blade assembly and extract old bearings using a puller
- Clean arbor shaft thoroughly with appropriate cleaning agents
- Press-fit new bearings into place (FAG 6210-2RS, 4 nos.) in the exact fit required by specifications
- Reassemble blades properly with spacers to maintain alignment
- Verify all components are correctly reassembled and secure

#### Preventive Recommendations:
- Implement a regular lubrication schedule that includes SKF LGMT 2 grease for bearings.
- Conduct vibration spectrum analysis monthly to detect overheating issues early.
- Replace arbor bearings at 5000 operating hours regardless of condition, including bearing life-based replacements when approaching service limits.
- Perform blade and arbor inspection before each shift and replace blades as needed to prevent material damage.

The downtime was 5.5 hours, with a mean time to repair (MTTR) of 4-6 hours. The production line was completely stopped for this incident, leading to significant losses in revenue and production efficiency.
