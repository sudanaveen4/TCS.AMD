The sudden increase in vibration (from 2.018 Hz to 3.981 Hz) coincides with a significant drop in main motor current (from 161.876 A to 158.461 A). This phenomenon is a direct indicator of motor bearing failure due to excessive wear or overbearing on the bearings.

### Contributing Factors:
- Vibration Peak: The sudden increase in vibration suggests that the machine was subjected to more intense vibrations, possibly indicating an imbalance or misalignment within the mechanical system.
- Reduced Motor Current: Decreased current is a common symptom of bearing failures as it indicates reduced lubrication and friction resistance between moving parts.

## Root Cause Analysis

### Direct Causes:
1. **Excessive Vibration**: The sudden increase in vibration (peak at 3.981 Hz) suggests that the machine was subjected to higher-than-normal vibrations, which could be due to misalignment or imbalance within the system.
2. **Decreased Motor Current**: The significant decrease in main motor current (from 161.876 A to 158.461 A) indicates reduced lubrication and friction resistance between moving parts.

### Contributing Factors:
3. **Excessive Heat Buildup**: Although not explicitly stated in the telemetry data, overheating of bearings can lead to increased vibration as a response mechanism from the system.
4. **Wear on Bearings**: Over time, continuous use may result in wear and tear on the bearings, leading to decreased friction resistance and reduced current.
