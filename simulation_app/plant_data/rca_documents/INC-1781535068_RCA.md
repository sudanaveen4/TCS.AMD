# Root Cause Analysis Report for Incident INC-1781535068

## Executive Summary
The incident on FRM-201A motor bearing failure (FM-101) occurred at 20:21:08 UTC. The primary cause was identified as a significant vibration increase and decreased main motor current, both of which are indicative of overbearing or excessive wear on the bearings within the mechanism. This report will explore these causes in detail and provide recommendations for prevention.

## Telemetry & Technical Analysis
From the telemetry slice provided:
- **Vibration Analysis**: The vibration readings increased significantly from around 1.99 to around 4.88, indicating an increase in mechanical stress and potential bearing fatigue.
- **Current Analysis**: The main motor current decreased slightly over time but remained above normal levels, suggesting that the load on the bearings was heavier than expected.

These changes are consistent with bearing failure or severe wear due to prolonged operation under excessive loads without proper maintenance. This highlights the importance of regular bearing inspection and timely replacement before catastrophic failures occur.

## Root Cause Analysis
### Direct Causes:
1. **Excessive Load**: The primary cause of the motor bearing failure is a significant increase in load on the bearings, leading to overheating and eventual failure.
2. **Vibration Patterns**: The increased vibration readings indicate that the bearings are experiencing more wear or have reached their critical operating limits.

### Contributing Causes:
1. **Maintenance Lapse**: There was no scheduled maintenance for the machinery within the timeframe of this incident. This lack of periodic inspection allowed for degradation to escalate without intervention.
2. **Overuse and Unchecked Wear**: Continuous operation under heavy load conditions led to accelerated bearing wear, resulting in a failure point that had been ignored due to perceived stability.

## Corrective Actions
The corrective actions taken following the RCA process include:
1. **Immediate Shutdown and Maintenance Check** - The machinery was shut down immediately after the incident to assess the condition of the bearings.
2. **Manual Inspection Report (t2)**: The machine was visually inspected by a technician, confirming that everything within the mechanism appeared normal upon sight. This manual verification is crucial in ruling out other possible causes.
3. **RCA Report Generation (t3)**: A Root Cause Analysis report was generated and reviewed by various stakeholders to document findings and identify any potential recurring issues.

## Preventive Recommendations
To prevent similar incidents in the future, consider implementing the following measures:
1. **Regular Maintenance Schedule**: Establish a routine maintenance schedule for all critical machinery components, including bearings.
2. **Operational Monitoring**: Implement continuous monitoring systems to detect early signs of wear or overload conditions that could indicate bearing failure.
3. **Training and Awareness Programs**: Educate operators on proper machine operation techniques and the importance of regular equipment inspections.

## Estimated Impact
The downtime for this incident amounted to approximately 5 minutes, resulting in a minimal production loss due to the short duration of the fault (6-hour shift). However, the immediate halt was crucial to prevent further damage which could have escalated into a more significant failure.
