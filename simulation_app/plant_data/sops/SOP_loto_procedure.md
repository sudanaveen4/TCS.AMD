## 1. PURPOSE
The purpose of this procedure is to protect personnel from injury caused by the unexpected energization, startup, or release of stored energy from machines, equipment, or processes during service, maintenance, or repair operations.

## 2. SCOPE
This procedure applies to all TCS Bharat Steel Pipes employees, contractors, and vendors performing maintenance, cleaning, unjamming, or setup on any equipment where unexpected energization could occur.

**EXCEPTIONS:**
- Minor tool changes or adjustments during normal production operations, provided they are routine, repetitive, and integral to the use of the equipment, AND provided effective alternative protection (like physical guards or safety interlocks) is used.
- Work on cord-and-plug-connected electrical equipment where the plug is under the exclusive control of the employee performing the service.

## 3. TYPES OF HAZARDOUS ENERGY
Before working on any machine, all of the following potential energy sources must be considered:
1. **Electrical:** Mains power (415V/11kV), control power (24V DC / 110V AC), UPS, capacitors (inverters)
2. **Hydraulic:** Pressurized oil (up to 250 bar)
3. **Pneumatic:** Compressed air (up to 7 bar)
4. **Mechanical:** Kinetic (rotating parts) or Potential (springs, elevated weights, gravity)
5. **Thermal:** High temperatures (furnace up to 950°C, weld up to 1400°C) or extreme cold
6. **Chemical:** Toxic, corrosive, or flammable substances (coolants, oils, gases)
7. **Radiation:** High frequency RF (Welder), Lasers (QC bay)

## 4. LOTO EQUIPMENT REQUIREMENTS
- **Personal Padlocks:** Red body, keyed individually. Only ONE key exists per lock. The key must be kept on the person who applied the lock.
- **LOTO Tags:** Standardized durable tags with the warning "DANGER - DO NOT OPERATE". Must include: Name, Date, Contact number, and Reason for LOTO.
- **Group Lockbox:** Used for complex maintenance involving multiple isolation points or multiple workers.
- **Isolation Devices:** Multi-lock hasps (scissor clips), circuit breaker lockouts, valve covers, pneumatic plug lockouts.

## 5. THE 6-STEP ISOLATION PROCEDURE

### Step 1: Prepare for Shutdown
- Identify the machine or equipment.
- Locate the specific equipment LOTO procedure (posted near the machine's main panel).
- Identify all energy sources and isolating devices.
- Notify the Shift Supervisor and all affected operators that the equipment is being shut down for maintenance.

### Step 2: Machine or Equipment Shutdown
- Shut down the machine using the normal stopping procedure (e.g., press stop button, turn off switch).
- Ensure all moving parts have come to a complete stop.

### Step 3: Equipment Isolation
- Operate all energy isolating devices so that the equipment is isolated from its energy sources.
  - *Example: Turn the main electrical disconnect switch to the OFF position.*
  - *Example: Close the main hydraulic supply valve.*

### Step 4: Apply LOTO Devices
- Apply the lock and tag to each energy-isolating device.
- Use a multi-lock hasp if more than one person will be working on the equipment.
- **Rule:** EVERY person working on the equipment MUST apply their own personal lock. Never work under someone else's lock.

### Step 5: Control Stored Energy
- Inspect the equipment for any stored or residual energy.
- Release, disconnect, or restrain any stored energy:
  - *Wait for capacitors to discharge (Minimum 15 mins for WLD-301 and HTR-401 inverters).*
  - *Bleed hydraulic/pneumatic pressure to zero using drain valves.*
  - *Block or physically support elevated components that could fall by gravity.*
  - *Allow thermal energy to dissipate to a safe temperature.*

### Step 6: Verify Isolation (TESTING)
- **CRITICAL STEP:** Verify that the equipment is completely de-energized.
- Clear all personnel from the hazard zone.
- Attempt to start the machine using the normal operating controls.
  - *Press the "Start" button. The machine MUST NOT start.*
- Return all operating controls to the "Neutral" or "Off" position after testing.
- For electrical work: Use a calibrated multimeter to verify zero voltage at the point of work. (Test Before Touch).

*The equipment is now locked out and safe to work on.*

## 6. RESTORE TO SERVICE PROCEDURE

When maintenance is complete and the equipment is ready to return to normal operation:

1. **Inspect the Work Area:** Ensure all tools have been removed, all guards have been replaced, and the equipment is fully reassembled.
2. **Ensure Personnel are Clear:** Check that all employees are safely positioned away from the equipment.
3. **Remove LOTO Devices:** Each person must remove their own lock and tag.
4. **Restore Energy:** Re-energize the equipment by opening valves, closing breakers, etc., in the reverse order of isolation.
5. **Notify Affected Employees:** Inform the Shift Supervisor and operators that the maintenance is complete and the equipment is ready for use.
6. **Test Run:** Operator to perform a test run before resuming full production.

## 7. SPECIAL SITUATIONS

### 7.1 Shift Change / Handover
If work extends beyond one shift, LOTO continuity must be maintained:
- **Direct Handover:** The oncoming worker applies their lock BEFORE the outgoing worker removes theirs.
- **No Direct Handover:** The outgoing worker leaves their personal lock on, OR replaces it with a special "Departmental Lock" (Yellow body). The Shift Supervisor holds the key to the Departmental Lock. The oncoming worker must apply their personal lock before the Departmental Lock is removed.

### 7.2 Emergency Lock Removal
If a lock is left on by an employee who has left the plant and cannot be reached:
1. ONLY the Plant Manager or HSE Manager can authorize removal.
2. The authorizer must physically verify the employee is not on-site.
3. The authorizer must physically inspect the equipment to ensure it is safe to energize.
4. The lock is cut off.
5. The employee MUST be informed before they resume work on their next shift that their lock was removed.

### 7.3 Group LOTO
For complex jobs requiring many isolation points:
1. The Primary Authorized Employee (Supervisor) locks out all energy sources using "Equipment Locks" (Blue body).
2. All keys for the Equipment Locks are placed inside a Group Lockbox.
3. Every worker assigned to the job places their Personal Lock (Red body) on the Group Lockbox.
4. The keys inside the box cannot be accessed until EVERY worker has finished and removed their personal lock.

## 8. ENFORCEMENT & VIOLATIONS
Failure to follow this LOTO procedure is a **Zero Tolerance safety violation**.
- 1st Offense: Immediate suspension pending investigation. Potential termination.
- Unauthorized removal of someone else's lock: Immediate termination.
