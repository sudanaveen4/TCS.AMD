## 3. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### 3.1 Fire Emergency
1. **DISCOVER** → Shout "FIRE" and alert nearby workers
2. **ALARM** → Press nearest fire alarm call point (red boxes at every zone exit)
3. **FIGHT** (only if safe and trained) → Use nearest fire extinguisher. Do not fight fire larger than a waste bin.
4. **EVACUATE** → Follow green exit signs to nearest assembly point
5. **REPORT** → Call Emergency Coordinator: **+91-9876543210** (Deepak Rao)

### 3.2 Medical Emergency
1. Call First Aider: **Ext: 555** or nearest trained first aider
2. Do not move injured person unless in immediate danger
3. Apply first aid from nearest first aid box (located at every zone entrance)
4. For serious injury: Call ambulance **108** and inform HSE Manager
5. First Aid Box Locations: Z1-Entry, Z2-Panel, Z3-Panel, Z4-Entry, Z5-Entry, Z6-Office, Z7-Workshop, Z9-Admin

### 3.3 Chemical / Oil Spill
1. Avoid contact with spilled material
2. Contain spill using spill kit (absorbent pads + sand)
3. Spill Kit Locations: Z3 (coolant), Z5 (water), Z7 (oil), Z8 (fuel/oil)
4. Notify Environmental Officer: **+91-9876543212** (Kavitha Reddy)
5. Do NOT wash spill into drains

### 3.4 Electrical Emergency
1. Do NOT touch the person if they are in contact with live conductors
2. Switch OFF power at nearest isolator or emergency stop
3. Call Electrical Supervisor: **+91-9876543211** (Sunil Prasad)
4. If person is free from electrical contact, start CPR if needed
5. For HV incidents (>415V): Evacuate 10m radius. Wait for qualified rescue.

## 5. LOCKOUT/TAGOUT (LOTO) PROCEDURE

### 5.1 When is LOTO Required?
- Any maintenance or repair work on machines
- Cleaning inside machine enclosures
- Removing or bypassing safety guards
- Working on electrical panels or wiring
- Hydraulic/pneumatic system work
- Any work where unexpected energization could cause injury

### 5.2 LOTO Steps (Sequential)
1. **NOTIFY** — Inform shift supervisor and all affected personnel
2. **IDENTIFY** — Identify all energy sources (electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, thermal, gravitational, chemical)
3. **SHUTDOWN** — Shut down machine using normal stop controls
4. **ISOLATE** — Open/turn OFF all energy isolation devices (main isolator, valve, breaker)
5. **LOCK** — Apply personal padlock on each isolation device. Only ONE lock per person.
6. **TAG** — Attach LOTO tag with: Name, Date, Time, Reason, Expected duration
7. **VERIFY** — Attempt to restart machine (must not start). Check zero energy:
   - Electrical: Use voltmeter to verify 0V
   - Hydraulic: Open bleed valve, verify 0 bar on gauge
   - Pneumatic: Open drain, verify 0 bar
   - Thermal: Wait until safe temperature or use thermal barriers
8. **WORK** — Perform maintenance work safely
9. **REMOVE** — Only the person who applied the lock may remove it
10. **RESTART** — Verify all guards in place, all personnel clear, then restart

### 5.3 LOTO Points Per Machine

| Machine | Electrical Isolation | Hydraulic | Pneumatic | Other |
|---------|---------------------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| CNC-101 | Main isolator (MCC) | HPU bleed valve | FRL drain valve | — |
| FRM-201 | Main isolator (MCC) | Stand adjustment bleed | — | Coolant pump isolator |
| WLD-301 | Main isolator (MCC) + 11kV breaker | Squeeze stand bleed | — | Cooling water valve |
