# HEALTH, SAFETY, AND ENVIRONMENT (HSE)
**Document Title**: Emergency Response & Evacuation Protocols
**Document ID**: HSE-ER-01
**Unit**: Crude Distillation Unit (CDU)

## 1. Loss of Containment (Hydrocarbon Leak)
If a high-pressure leak occurs in the CDU (e.g., failed pump mechanical seal, ruptured heat exchanger tube):
1. **Detection**: LEL (Lower Explosive Limit) gas detectors will trigger at 20% LEL.
2. **Immediate Action**: 
   * The AI Copilot / Control Room Operator must trigger an Emergency Shutdown (ESD-1) to isolate the affected segment.
   * Activate the deluge fire-water system over the leaking equipment to prevent ignition.
3. **Evacuation**: Sound the plant-wide alarm. All non-essential personnel must evacuate to Assembly Point Alpha (upwind of the CDU).

## 2. Furnace Tube Rupture
A rupture inside the Fired Heater releases high-pressure crude oil directly into the firebox, causing an immense internal fire and thick black smoke from the stack.
1. **Immediate Action**:
   * Trigger the Master Fuel Trip (MFT) to cut all fuel gas to the burners.
   * IMMEDIATELY open the manual snuffing steam valves to flood the firebox with steam and smother the fire.
   * Stop the crude charge pumps.
2. **Post-Incident**: Do NOT open the furnace doors until the internal temperature is below 150°C. Introducing oxygen to a hot firebox will cause an explosion.

## 3. Loss of Cooling Water / Power Failure
A total loss of utilities requires a safe, controlled shutdown of the entire unit.
1. All control valves will fail to their safe state (e.g., fuel gas valves fail CLOSED, flare valves fail OPEN).
2. The Distillation Column will rapidly over-pressurize as the overhead condensers fail.
3. Operators must ensure the Safety Relief Valves (SRVs) are lifting and routing gas safely to the flare header. 
4. Monitor the flare stack for excessive liquid carryover.
