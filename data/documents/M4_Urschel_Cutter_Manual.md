# Urschel Precision Rotary Cutter R-5 Manual
**Manufacturer**: Urschel Laboratories
**Machine Type**: High-Speed Centrifugal Slicer
**Location**: Zone Z4 (Cutting & Assembly)

## 1. System Overview
The Urschel R-5 uses centrifugal force to push the continuous, cooled meat-analog slab against a high-speed rotating cutting head, producing exact strips, dices, or shreds.

![Urschel Cutter Diagram](./images/urschel_cutter_diagram.png)

## 2. Operating Parameters
- **Normal RPM (Cutting Head)**: 1400 - 1500 RPM
- **Normal Throughput**: 400 - 460 kg/h
- **Motor Current**: 8.0 A - 12.0 A
- **Normal Vibration Limit**: < 3.5 mm/s

## 3. Maintenance & Troubleshooting

### 3.1 Imbalance / Blade Wear
- **Symptoms**: Vibration creeps upward from 2.0 mm/s to > 5.0 mm/s. 
- **Action**: The cutting blades are either dull, chipped, or improperly torqued, causing a centrifugal imbalance. Halt operations and replace the entire blade assembly. *Do not replace individual blades; replace as a balanced set.*

### 3.2 Mechanical Jam
- **Symptoms**: Motor current spikes above 20 A. RPM drops to 0. Throughput is 0.
- **Action**: A dense, un-texturized chunk from the extruder has jammed the slicing head. Lock out the machine, open the impeller housing, and manually remove the obstruction.

## 4. Safety 
- **Laceration Hazard**: Urschel blades are razor sharp. Cut-resistant Kevlar gloves MUST be worn when handling the cutting head.
- **Interlock Switch**: Never bypass the magnetic safety interlock on the impeller cover.
