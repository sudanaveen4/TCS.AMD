# STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)
**Document Title**: Fired Heater (Furnace) Operations & Safety
**Document ID**: CDU-SOP-02
**Unit**: Crude Distillation Unit (CDU)
**Revision**: 2.1

## 1. Overview
The Fired Heater raises the temperature of the crude oil from the Heat Exchanger Network (approx 220°C) to the flash zone temperature (350°C) required for atmospheric distillation.

![Fired Heater Diagram](./images/fired_heater_diagram.png)

## 2. Safety Systems
* **Burner Management System (BMS)**: The furnace is fully interlocked. Any failure in draft pressure, fuel gas pressure, or flame detection will trigger a master fuel trip (MFT).
* **Snuffing Steam**: In the event of a tube rupture, snuffing steam must be manually or automatically injected to displace oxygen and prevent a catastrophic explosion.

## 3. Lighting Procedures
1. **Purge**: Run the induced draft (ID) fans at 60% for exactly 15 minutes to clear the firebox of unburnt hydrocarbons.
2. **Pilots**: Ignite the pilots using the electronic igniter system. Visually verify all 8 pilot flames through the viewports.
3. **Main Burners**: Introduce fuel gas to the main burners sequentially. 

## 4. Operating Parameters & Alarms
- **Radiant Tube Skin Temperature**: Normal 450°C. High Alarm: 500°C. *Warning: Exceeding 520°C causes rapid coking and tube metallurgical failure.*
- **Draft Pressure**: Maintain -15 mmH2O. 
- **Flue Gas O2**: Target 2.5% - 3.5%.

## 5. Abnormal Conditions
* **Flame Impingement**: If flames are directly touching the tubes, immediately reduce draft and adjust the burner air registers. Continued impingement leads to localized hot spots and tube rupture.
* **High Tube Skin Temperature**: Indicates severe internal coking. The crude flow must be increased to provide cooling, or the furnace must be taken offline for decoking (steam-air decoking procedure).
