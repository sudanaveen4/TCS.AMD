# MAINTENANCE PROCEDURE
**Document Title**: Shell & Tube Heat Exchanger Fouling Management
**Document ID**: MAINT-HEX-02
**Asset Class**: Heat Exchanger Network (HEN)

## 1. Overview
The Heat Exchanger Network recovers heat from distillation column products to pre-heat the incoming crude oil. Over time, asphaltenes, salts, and inorganic compounds deposit on the tube walls, reducing heat transfer efficiency (fouling).

![Heat Exchanger Diagram](./images/heat_exchanger_diagram.png)

## 2. Detection of Fouling
* **Temperature Pinch**: A decrease in the temperature difference between the hot and cold streams. If the crude outlet temperature drops by > 5°C over a 30-day period under constant flow, significant fouling is present.
* **Pressure Drop (ΔP)**: An increase in differential pressure across the tube or shell side. 
  * *Warning*: ΔP > 1.5 barg indicates severe blockage.

## 3. Cleaning Procedures
When thermal efficiency drops below 70%, cleaning is required.

### 3.1 Chemical Cleaning (Online/Offline)
* Inject chemical dispersants into the crude feed to solubilize asphaltenes. This is a temporary measure to extend the run-length before a mechanical turnaround.

### 3.2 High-Pressure Hydroblasting (Offline)
1. **Isolation**: Isolate, drain, and steam-out the heat exchanger.
2. **Bundle Pulling**: Remove the channel head and pull the tube bundle using an aerial bundle extractor.
3. **Hydroblasting**: Clean the internal tubes and exterior shell side using a 10,000 psi to 20,000 psi hydroblasting lance.
4. **Inspection**: Perform Eddy Current testing (ECT) on a 10% sample of the tubes to check for localized pitting or wall thinning before reassembly.
