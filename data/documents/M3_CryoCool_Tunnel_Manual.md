# CryoCool Tunnel C-40 Operations Manual
**Manufacturer**: Air Products
**Machine Type**: Cryogenic Cooling Tunnel / Cooling Die
**Location**: Zone Z3 (Cooling & Treatment)

## 1. System Overview
The CryoCool Tunnel is responsible for the rapid texturization of the extruded protein. The hot melt (140°C) from the extruder is forced into the cooling die, where controlled cooling causes the protein fibers to align longitudinally, creating a meat-like texture. 

![CryoCool Tunnel Diagram](./images/cryocool_tunnel_diagram.png)

## 2. Operating Parameters
- **Normal RPM (Conveyor)**: Variable (Matched to extruder output)
- **Normal Throughput**: 450 - 500 kg/h
- **Internal Temperature Profile**: Drops from 140°C at entry to 60°C at exit.
- **Cooling Medium**: Glycol / Chilled Water loop.
- **Normal Vibration Limit**: < 2.0 mm/s

## 3. Maintenance & Troubleshooting

### 3.1 Conveyor Belt Slippage
- **Symptoms**: Throughput drops suddenly without a corresponding drop in motor RPM. Current remains low.
- **Action**: The pneumatic tensioners have failed or the belt is coated in oil/fat. Clean the belt and verify tensioner pressure (Min 4.0 Bar).

### 3.2 Cooling Blockage
- **Symptoms**: Exit temperature exceeds 80°C. The product exits as a soft mush instead of a fibrous structure.
- **Action**: Check the chilled water flow rate. A blocked heat exchanger or failed circulation pump is the likely cause.

## 4. Safety Procedures
- **Slip Hazard**: Condensation around the tunnel can make floors extremely slippery.
- **Pinch Points**: Keep hands clear of the conveyor ingress points.
